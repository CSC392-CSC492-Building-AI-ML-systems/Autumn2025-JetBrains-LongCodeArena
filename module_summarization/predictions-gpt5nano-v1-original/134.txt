Annihilation in Alchemical Transformations
===========================================

Overview
--------
Alchemical transformations enable the gradual decoupling or coupling of atoms between two chemical states during a molecular simulation. This is accomplished by constructing a hybrid topology that blends an old (reference) topology with a new (target) topology and by modulating interactions with a coupling parameter, lambda. The HybridTopologyFactory in this codebase encapsulates the creation and management of such a hybrid system and its associated metadata.

Atom classes and lambda behavior
-------------------------------
Atoms are categorized into four classes, which determine how their interactions are treated as lambda varies:

- unique_old_atom
  - Not mapped to the new topology.
  - Present only in the old system.
  - Interactions are on at lambda = 0 and off at lambda = 1.

- unique_new_atom
  - Not mapped to the old topology.
  - Present only in the new system.
  - Interactions are off at lambda = 0 and on at lambda = 1.

- core_atom
  - Mapped between old and new systems and part of a changing residue.
  - Interactions follow the old system at lambda = 0 and the new system at lambda = 1.

- environment_atom
  - Mapped between topologies but not part of a changing residue.
  - Interactions are always active and are alchemically unmodified.

Hybrid topology and mappings
----------------------------
The factory produces and exposes:

- hybrid_system: the OpenMM System representing the blended (hybrid) topology.
- new_to_hybrid_atom_map: mapping from new-system atoms to hybrid atoms.
- old_to_hybrid_atom_map: mapping from old-system atoms to hybrid atoms.
- hybrid_positions: the positions for the atoms in the hybrid system.
- hybrid_topology: topology of the hybrid system (mdtraj Topology).
- omm_hybrid_topology: OpenMM Topology object corresponding to the hybrid system.

Notes:
- This API is experimental and subject to change.

Forces and energy terms
-----------------------
The hybrid system includes a subset of forces that may be alchemically transformed. The class keeps track of the forces it can handle:

- HarmonicBondForce
- HarmonicAngleForce
- PeriodicTorsionForce
- NonbondedForce
- MonteCarloBarostat (when present)

These forces are scaled with lambda according to a user-supplied functions dictionary.

Initialization and configuration
-------------------------------
Key parameters supplied to the HybridTopologyFactory include:

- topology_proposal: a topology proposal object (e.g., from a topology proposal engine) describing old and new topologies.
- current_positions: coordinates for the old system.
- new_positions: coordinates for the new system.
- use_dispersion_correction: whether to apply a long-range dispersion correction for the sterics portion.
- functions: a dictionary defining how each force term is scaled with lambda. Keys must follow the pattern lambda_<force> and must cover bonds, angles, torsions, sterics, and electrostatics if provided.
- softcore_alpha: softcore parameter controlling the shape of the softcore interaction (defaults applied if None).
- bond_softening_constant, angle_softening_constant: factors to soften bonds involving unique atoms and unique-core atoms at the endpoints of the alchemical transition.
- softcore_LJ_v2: whether to use the LJ v2 softcore formulation (recommended).
- softcore_electrostatics: whether to apply softcore electrostatics.
- softcore_LJ_v2_alpha, softcore_electrostatics_alpha: softcore alpha parameters for LJ and electrostatics.
- softcore_sigma_Q: softcore sigma parameter for electrostatics.
- interpolate_old_and_new_14s: whether to turn off 1-4 interactions for new atoms at lambda=0 and for old atoms at lambda=1 (default behavior is to leave these interactions present in the nonbonded force).
- omitted_terms: dictionary of terms (by new topology index) that must be annealed in over a lambda protocol.
- flatten_torsions: if True, torsions involving unique_new_atoms are scaled such that they are off at lambda=0 and on at lambda=1 (and the opposite for unique_old_atoms).
- rmsd_restraint: whether to apply an RMSD restraint between core heavy atoms and protein Cα atoms.
- endstate: parameter guiding the lambda endstate behavior. For this HybridTopologyFactory, endstate must be None (it should be 0 or 1 for a different factory, the RepartitionedHybridTopologyFactory). The constructor will error if an endstate of 0 or 1 is provided.

Operational notes
-------------------
- The alchemical transformation is commonly performed by visiting lambda values from 0 to 1 (forward) or 1 to 0 (reverse), with the four atom classes adjusting their interactions according to the rules above.
- The functions dictionary allows precise control over how each force term is scaled with lambda, enabling custom transformation protocols for bonds, angles, torsions, sterics, and electrostatics.
- The use of softcore potentials (LJ and electrostatics) avoids singularities when decoupling or coupling atoms during annihilation/creation.

Warnings
-------
- The API is experimental and may change in future releases. Users should verify compatibility when upgrading.

Endstate guidance
-----------------
- For HybridTopologyFactory, endstate should be None. The endstate parameter exists for other factories (e.g., RepartitionedHybridTopologyFactory) where an explicit lambda endstate is required.
