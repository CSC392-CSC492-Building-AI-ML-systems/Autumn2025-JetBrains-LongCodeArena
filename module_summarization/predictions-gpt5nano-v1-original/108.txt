Polys
===== 
Polynomial factorization routines in characteristic zero.

Overview
--------
This module provides core factorization routines for polynomials over characteristic zero domains. It exposes a mixin class that implements several factorization strategies used by higher-level polynomial rings, including trial-divison based factorization, algebraic-number-field factorization, and Zassenhaus/Enhanced EEZ-based factorization for multivariate and univariate polynomials over Z. The routines are designed to integrate with the surrounding domain/ring infrastructure and honor configuration options controlled by the query() system.

API
---
Class: _Factor
Mixin base class for factorization routines. The class assumes the existence of a domain-aware polynomial interface and a collection of helper routines in the surrounding package (e.g., factorization helpers, domain conversions, and factoring strategies). The following methods are implemented:

- _trial_division(self, f, factors)
  - Description: Perform trial division of a polynomial f by a list of candidate factors.
  - Parameters:
    - f: The polynomial to factor.
    - factors: A sequence of polynomials representing potential irreducible factors.
  - Returns: A sorted list of pairs (factor, multiplicity).

  - Notes: Uses _sort_factors to combine repeated factors and sort the result.

- _aa_factor_trager(self, f)
  - Description: Factor multivariate polynomials over algebraic number fields.
  - Returns: A tuple (lc, factors) where lc is the leading coefficient of the monic form and factors is a list of irreducible factors with multiplicities.

  - Details: 
    - Handles ground polynomials (constants) by returning the leading coefficient and no factors.
    - Uses square-free factorization and cofactorization (cofactors) in conjunction with a transformation of generators.

- _zz_factor(self, f)
  - Description: Factor (non-square-free) polynomials in Z[X].
  - Returns: A tuple (content, [(fi, ki)...]) representing the content and the irreducible factors with multiplicities.

  - Key behavior:
    - For univariate polynomials, reduces to primitive part factoring and may apply irreducibility or cyclotomic checks (via configuration flags) before using Zassenhaus/EEZ-based factoring.
    - For multivariate polynomials, reduces to primitive univariate components and recursively factors subcomponents, combining results with the primitive part.

  - Notes: This method is central to integer-coefficient polynomial factorization and leverages various subroutines (e.g., zassenhaus, wang lifting, cyclotomic factoring) based on configuration.

- _zz_mignotte_bound(self, f)
  - Description: Compute the Mignotte bound for multivariate polynomials in Z[X].
  - Returns: A bound (domain element) used in factorization to limit the candidate factor sizes.

  - Details: Uses max_norm, degree sums across generators, and leading coefficient information.

- _zz_zassenhaus(self, f)
  - Description: Factor primitive square-free polynomials in Z[x].
  - Returns: A list of factors (as polynomials) for the univariate case.

  - Notes:
    - Assumes the polynomial is univariate and the domain is the Integer Ring.
    - Employs Mignotte bounds, prime selection (to ensure square-freeness in Z_p), Hensel lifting, and subsequent combinatorial lifting to reconstruct factors in Z.

Configuration and Requirements
------------------------------
- The routines rely on:
  - A domain-aware polynomial representation with attributes such as is_univariate, primitive(), LC, monic(), is_ground, max_norm(), degree(), sqf_part(), factor_list(), etc.
  - A global query() mechanism to toggle strategies, e.g.:
    - USE_IRREDUCIBLE_IN_FACTOR
    - USE_CYCLOTOMIC_FACTOR
- External helpers and structures:
  - factorint, isprime, nextprime (number theory)
  - symmetric_residue (modular arithmetic support)
  - Coercion/DomainError and related polyerrors
  - _sort_factors (ordering and deduplication helper)

Examples
--------
- Univariate factorization in ZZ:
  >>> _, x = ring('x', ZZ)
  >>> (2*x**4 - 2).factor_list()
  (2, [(x - 1, 1), (x + 1, 1), (x**2 + 1, 1)])

- Multivariate/univariate hybrid factorization:
  >>> _, x, y = ring('x y', ZZ)
  >>> (2*x**2 - 2*y**2).factor_list()
  (2, [(x - y, 1), (x + y, 1)])

Notes
-----
- The implementation integrates with higher-level factorization logic in the surrounding polynomial package and relies on a mix of classical (Zassenhaus) and modern (EEZ) strategies.
- The algorithms are designed to be robust for polynomials over the integers and extend to multivariate cases through lifting and cofactorization steps.
- Detailed references include standard texts on polynomial factorization in Z[X], including works such as Gathen and Gerhard (Gathen1999modern).

References
