Three types of algorithms for the estimation of the parameters of a model
==========================================================================

This section documents the three broad families of optimization algorithms exposed by the generic fitting interface implemented in Optimizer._fit. Each family maps to a set of methods that can be selected via the method argument. The available methods are:

- newton, ncg, bfgs, lbfgs, cg
- nm, powell
- basinhopping
- minimize (wrapper for scipy.optimize.minimize)

Local gradient-based optimization
---------------------------------

These methods use gradient information (and, where applicable, a Hessian) to locate the minimum (or maximum) of the objective function. They typically converge quickly near the optimum when the objective is smooth and well-behaved.

Supported methods:
- newton: Newton-Raphson style updates (requires gradient and sometimes Hessian)
- ncg: Newton-CG (Newton-Conjugate-Gradient)
- bfgs: Broyden-Fletcher-Goldfarb-Shanno update
- lbfgs: Limited-memory BFGS
- cg: Conjugate gradient

Notes:
- These methods generally require a gradient function (score) and may use a Hessian (or an approximation).
- They are suitable for well-behaved, differentiable objectives with a good starting point.

Derivative-free local optimization
-------------------------------

These methods do not rely on gradient information and can be more robust to non-smooth or noisy objectives. They may be slower to converge but can be more reliable when derivatives are unavailable or unreliable.

Supported methods:
- nm: Nelder-Mead simplex method
- powell: Powell’s method

Notes:
- Useful when derivatives are unavailable or when the objective is not differentiable.
- Convergence can be slower and may require careful tuning of tolerances and starting points.

Global optimization
-------------------

Global optimization methods aim to search broadly across the parameter space to escape local minima and increase the chances of finding the global minimum. They are often used in conjunction with a local solver.

Supported method:
- basinhopping: global basin-hopping strategy that combines random perturbations with a local minimizer

Notes:
- Basinhopping ignores some local solver settings (maxiter, retall, full_output) in favor of its own global-search loop.
- It can be configured with parameters such as niter, niter_success, T, stepsize, interval, minimizer (the local optimizer to use), and other options mapped to the underlying local minimizer (e.g., the 'minimize' interface).

General interface and options
------------------------------

- The fit method accepts:
  - objective, gradient, start_params, fargs, kwargs
  - hessian (optional)
  - method (one of: 'newton', 'nm', 'bfgs', 'powell', 'cg', 'ncg', 'basinhopping', 'minimize')
  - maxiter, full_output, disp, callback, retall

- Basinhopping is designed to work in tandem with a local minimizer and provides its own global-search behavior, while the other methods rely on gradient or derivative-free local optimization.

- The exact output depends on the solver chosen, but the interface generally returns the optimized parameters, solver-specific information, and the settings used for the optimization.
