polys.rst

Polynomial factorization routines in characteristic zero
======================================================


Overview
--------

This module provides low-level routines for factoring polynomials over characteristic zero
domains (notably over the integers Z and multivariate polynomial rings over Z). It
implements several classical algorithms:

- Zassenhaus and Enhanced Extended Zassenhaus (EEZ) for factoring polynomials in Z[X]
  (and multivariate generalizations via primitive factors and content extraction).
- Trager-style algebraic-number-field factorization for multivariate polynomials (aa
  factorization).
- Auxiliary utilities such as trial division to recover multiplicities of factors and
  bounds (Mignotte) used during lifting steps.

The implementation emphasizes a mixin class interface (_Factor) that provides the core
factorization helpers used by higher-level ring/factor logic in the surrounding library.
Configuration flags (via query) influence the factoring strategy, e.g., whether to test
irreducibility in factors or to attempt cyclotomic factorization first.


Module structure
----------------

- _Factor (mixin)
  - _trial_division(self, f, factors)
    - Performs trial division of a polynomial f by a list of candidate factors,
      returning a sorted list of (factor, multiplicity) pairs.
  - _aa_factor_trager(self, f)
    - Algebraic-number-field based factorization for multivariate polynomials.
      Returns a leading coefficient and a list of factors with multiplicities.
  - _zz_factor(self, f)
    - Primary Zassenhaus/EEZ factoring routine for polynomials over Z.
    - Handles univariate (Z[X]) and multivariate cases, returning (content, factors).
  - _zz_mignotte_bound(self, f)
    - Mignotte bound used in Zassenhaus-based lifting for multivariate cases.
  - _zz_zassenhaus(self, f)
    - Zassenhaus factoring for primitive, square-free univariate polynomials over Z.
      Implements prime selection, Hensel lifting, and splitting via combinations.


Key methods (descriptions)
--------------------------

- _trial_division(self, f, factors)
  - Input: a polynomial f and a list of candidate factors (with their current multiplicities).
  - Output: a sorted list of (factor, multiplicity) representing the factorization of f by the given candidates.
  - Purpose: recover exact multiplicities after lifting or splitting steps.

- _aa_factor_trager(self, f)
  - Input: a multivariate polynomial f over a domain with an algebraic-number-field factorization
    path available.
  - Output: a tuple (lc, factors) where lc is the leading coefficient and factors is a list of
    (factor, multiplicity) pairs.
  - Notes: factors are refined by cofactors and a translation in the algebraic setting. This
    implements the Trager-style approach for algebraic factorization.

- _zz_factor(self, f)
  - Input: a polynomial f in Z[X] or in a multivariate ring over Z, depending on the current
    ring structure (domain).
  - Output: a tuple (content, factors) where content is the content of f in Z and
    factors is a list of (factor, multiplicity) in the appropriate domain.
  - Behavior:
    - Univariate case (Z[X]):
      - Uses primitive part, optional irreducibility test, optional cyclotomic factorization,
        and Zassenhaus/EEZ factorization. Then applies trial division to recover multiplicities.
    - Multivariate case:
      - Extracts content, reduces to primitive part, factors the primitive part via
        a sequence of projections and lifting (including Wang lifting for nontrivial factors),
        and recursively factors factors from the specialized rings.
  - References internal configuration knobs:
    - USE_IRREDUCIBLE_IN_FACTOR
    - USE_CYCLOTOMIC_FACTOR
  - Notes: docstring examples illustrate typical outputs for classic polynomials over ZZ.

- _zz_mignotte_bound(self, f)
  - Input: a polynomial f in Z[X] or a multivariate analogue.
  - Output: an integer bound used to control lifting and the Hensel-lift step sizes.
  - Uses the maximum norm, degree information, and leading coefficient to compute a bound.

- _zz_zassenhaus(self, f)
  - Input: a primitive square-free polynomial f in Z[x] (univariate required).
  - Output: a list of irreducible factors of f in Z[x], together with their multiplicities.
  - Procedure:
    - Compute a Mignotte bound and a prime p where f remains square-free modulo p.
    - Hensel-lift the factorization modulo p to a candidate factorization over Z.
    - Use lifting information to split factors and verify divisibility of the original polynomial.
  - Notes: includes a carefully chosen prime search, bound computations, and lifting logic.

- _zz_zassenhaus (univariate, Z[X] primitive case) and _zz_factor (general case)
  - The univariate branch is a direct implementation of Zassenhaus/EEZ logic for primitive polynomials in Z[x].
  - The multivariate branch handles decomposition by reducing to primitive parts and recursing on subproblems.

Algorithms and design notes
---------------------------

- Zassenhaus/EEZ (for Z[X] and multivariate generalizations)
  - Core idea: factor a primitive polynomial in Z[x] by reducing modulo primes where the reduction is square-free, lifting the factorization back to Z using Hensel lifting, and then combining factors via combinatorial searches on subsets.
  - The implementation computes a bound (Mignotte bound) to guide the lifting process and uses trial division to assemble the final factorization with multiplicities.

- Trager approach (aa_factor_trager)
  - Factor multivariate polynomials using algebraic-number-field techniques and cofactors, followed by generic lifts back to the original polynomial ring.

- Cyclotomic optimization
  - If configured, the factorization path may attempt cyclotomic factorization before applying Zassenhaus, potentially simplifying the factor structure.

- Bounds and lifting
  - Mignotte bounds govern the size of coefficients during lifting.
  - Hensel lifting lifts factor information from a finite field back to integers.

Configuration flags (via query)
--------------------------------

- USE_IRREDUCIBLE_IN_FACTOR
  - If enabled, test irreducibility of certain factors to avoid unnecessary splits or to short-circuit certain factorizations.

- USE_CYCLOTOMIC_FACTOR
  - If enabled, attempt cyclotomic factorization as a preprocessing step before standard Zassenhaus.

Examples
--------

Univariate factorization over ZZ
--------------------------------

>>> _, x = ring('x', ZZ)
>>> (2*x**4 - 2).factor_list()
(2, [(x - 1, 1), (x + 1, 1), (x**2 + 1, 1)])

Multivariate factorization over ZZ
----------------------------------

>>> _, x, y = ring('x y', ZZ)
>>> (2*x**2 - 2*y**2).factor_list()
(2, [(x - y, 1), (x + y, 1)])

Notes
-----

- The examples show typical usage through the factoring interface provided by the repository (e.g., ring with ZZ as the base domain).
- The _Factor class is an internal mixin; public usage routes through higher-level factoring APIs in the library.
